## Objective

Add a `BasisExtractor` to the breach analysis framework so that perp-spot premium (basis) can be used as a stop-hunt discriminator. The hypothesis: stop hunts show a specific basis signature (perp moves more than spot during the wick, then snaps back), while real breakdowns move perp and spot together. The goal is to test whether basis-derived signals beat the current G_beyond_only champion model (F1 = 0.81 on tick labels).

## Decisions made (and why)

- **Use `market_candle` for perp, `breach_spot_candle` for spot** — decision made between sessions. Reason: keep the 12M+ rows of research-only spot data out of the production candle table. Perp data in `market_candle` is the legitimate production source and should remain shared. The percentile query in `_basis_percentile_24h` joins the two tables.

- **Basis units = basis points (bps)** — `(perp - spot) / spot * 10000`. Reason: human-readable and matches industry convention. Typical basis < 100 bps.

- **Direction-normalised stop-hunt signal** — for long stops (direction='below'), a basis DROP during breach is the stop-hunt signature, so we flip the sign. For short stops (direction='above'), we keep the sign. Stored as `basis_hunt_30s` and `basis_hunt_60s` in `build_signals()`.

- **Invert basis_hunt in model weights** — in the weighted models (Q_, R_, S_, T_), the basis_hunt weights are NEGATIVE: a strong stop-hunt signature should REDUCE the confirmation score (since stop hunts should be REJECTED, not confirmed).

- **NULL fallback for NO_SPOT symbols** — `has_spot: false` + all basis fields null. Reason: 30 of 84 symbols don't have a Bybit spot pair and never will. We want the extractor to produce a clean NULL rather than a synthetic value.

## Rejected approaches (and why)

- **Mark price integration** — briefly considered using mark price instead of last price to match Bybit's stop triggers. User clarified they use last price for all orders, so this is off the table.

- **Spot tick-level data instead of 1m candles** — considered but rejected as excessive data volume for modest resolution gain. 1m candle close gives us ≤60s staleness on spot, acceptable for a regime-context signal.

- **Trade-journal-driven mdsync discovery for backfill** — using `mdsync_pg` natively would require inserting synthetic spot trade_idea rows to get Phase A to pick up the symbols. Rejected as too invasive. Used direct `CandleFetcher` + `CandleStorePG` calls instead.

- **Without `--fast-load`** — first backfill attempt ran at ~250 candles/sec (20 hours total). Switched to fast-load with pre-dropped indexes, then got 280k candles in ~2 min per symbol. Kept this approach.
